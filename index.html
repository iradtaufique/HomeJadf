The Joint Action Development Forum (JADF)

Multi-stakeholder platform that facilitate and promote full participation of citizens in the decentralized and participatory governance and improve service provision processes with representatives from the public sector, private sector and civil society.

JADF STRUCTURE
RWANDA GORNANCE BORD

The organizational chart of JADF is designed hierarchically based on its objectives and strategy. JADF has its lines of communication and authority within the hierarchy, and allocates its defined duties and rights here. Within each hierarchy are specified roles, responsibilities and power. The hierarchy also shows how information flows as well as how responsibilities are delegated and coordinated.

Provinces/ City of Kigali

The role Provinces is to facilitate the sharing of good practices by providing a platform for stakeholders’ consultation where different Districts are benchmarked on how they are supporting the implementation of JADF policy. As a result of that coordinated dialogue on JADF strengthening, synergies between JADF member organizations are developed for greater impact.

Districts’ JADF

The District and Sector Authorities participates in JADF as members. Though JADF organs (General Assembly, Executive Committee, Thematic Commissions) they influence the establishment and proper functioning of the JADF partnership at district and sector level. The day-to-day management and monitoring of JADF operations are carried out by JADF Secretaries hosted in Districts’ Administrative premises. Respective core roles and responsibilities of JADF stakeholders are established by Prime Minister’s Instructions n°003/03 of 03/07/2015 establishing the joint action development forum and determining its responsibilities, organization and functioning.
